1. Course Description

In this course, you will explore cutting-edge nondestructive evaluation (NDE) methods used across industries from aerospace and construction to healthcare and energy. The course will help you discover how the hidden flaws and weaknesses in materials and structures are revealed without causing damage, and unlock the techniques that keep the world safe. Students will learn how to detect cracks, corrosion, and other defects using advanced technologies like ultrasound, X-rays, and terahertz radiation. This course goes beyond technical skills by allowing you to develop critical thinking, problem-solving, and analytical abilities essential to the modern engineer or scientist. The knowledge you gain here is indispensable, helping you ensure safety and reliability in systems that shape our society infrastructures such as bridges, airplanes, pipelines, and more. 2. Learning Objectives

  • Understand the fundamental concepts of Nondestructive Evaluation (NDE) and its importance across multiple industries.
  • Differentiate between various NDE techniques and analyze their effectiveness in identifying specific defects in materials.
  • Evaluate and select appropriate NDE methods for specific engineering scenarios.
  • Apply NDE knowledge in practical scenarios, focusing on terahertz testing for CFRP materials.

3. Assessment Plan

Your assessment will focus on practical application of NDE techniques through group activities, case studies, and quizzes. Key areas evaluated include understanding of NDE principles, ability to select and justify appropriate NDE methods, and clear communication of findings in reports and presentations.

4. Sample Exam/Major Assignment

Project: Nondestructive Testing Method Selection and Application

Objective: Assess the ability to apply NDT techniques in real-world scenarios. Students must select and justify an NDT method for critical infrastructure components, develop a procedure, and address potential risks.

Deliverables: Written report (1500-2000 words) and a 5-10 minute presentation. Evaluation will be based on research quality, practical justification, and detail in procedural steps.

5. Sample Lesson Plan

Lesson: Introduction to Nondestructive Testing Techniques for Engineering Problems

Objectives:

  • Understand the principles and applications of NDT, with emphasis on terahertz testing.
  • Differentiate between common NDT techniques.
  • Analyze case studies to determine suitable NDT methods for engineering challenges.

Activities include pre-reading, multimedia demonstrations, group discussions, and a reflective assignment to apply NDT techniques practically.

6. Syllabus

The syllabus will cover fundamentals of Nondestructive Evaluation, methods, and applications, with a focus on terahertz testing for CFRP materials. Topics include:

  • Week 1-2: Introduction to NDE and CFRP materials.
  • Week 3-5: Overview of NDE methods including ultrasonic, radiographic, and terahertz testing.
  • Week 6-8: Practical applications and case studies.
  • Week 9-10: Project work and presentations.
